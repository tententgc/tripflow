# .impeccable.md — TripFlow Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: Thai travelers (age 25-55) on group tours worldwide, using the PWA during trips — often in unfamiliar countries, sometimes without reliable internet (especially in China)
- **Secondary**: Thai tour operators managing trips via the admin portal
- **Context**: Users open the app many times a day while traveling — checking today's schedule, finding the hotel address, looking up emergency contacts, splitting dinner bills. They're often tired, jet-lagged, or in a hurry. The app must be instantly legible and calming, not demanding.
- **Job to be done**: "I want to know exactly what's happening today and feel confident I won't miss anything on this trip."

### Brand Personality
**Premium, Elegant, Polished**

- Voice: Reassuring, concise, respectful — like a well-trained concierge, not a chatbot
- Tone: Warm but refined. Never overly casual or corporate
- Emotional goals: Confidence, calm, delight, safety — travelers should feel the app has their back

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual Tone**: Apple-meets-Airbnb — clean whitespace, warm photography, soft rounded cards, premium typography. Southeast Asian practicality (Grab/LINE influence) for navigation density and service access patterns.

**Theme**: Light mode only. Warm cream base (`#faf8f5`) rather than cold white. Orange (`#f97316`) as primary accent — evokes warmth, travel, sunsets.

**References**:
- Apple Maps / Apple Travel — minimal cards, beautiful hierarchy, confident whitespace
- Airbnb — warm image-forward cards, rounded corners, friendly but not childish
- Grab / LINE — practical bottom nav, service grid, mobile-first density

**Anti-references** (must NOT look like):
- Cluttered travel agency websites — no busy banners, flashing deals, info overload
- Corporate enterprise dashboards — no stiff tables, no personality-free UI
- Overly playful / cartoon — no excessive illustrations, childish animations
- Dark / techy — no developer-tool aesthetic, dark backgrounds, monospace fonts

### Design Principles

1. **Calm Confidence** — The interface should feel like everything is under control. No visual noise, no competing calls-to-action. Information hierarchy does the heavy lifting.

2. **Warm Premium** — Soft shadows, warm neutrals (`#faf8f5`, `#2c1810`, `#b8a394`), generous whitespace. The app should feel like a luxury travel concierge, not a budget tool.

3. **Glanceable First** — Travelers check the app in 3-second bursts. Key info (time, place, meal) must be scannable without reading. Use icons, color coding, and spatial hierarchy.

4. **Touch-Friendly Always** — 44px minimum targets, generous spacing, forgiving hit areas. Users are walking, on buses, holding bags. Fat-finger tolerance is a feature.

5. **Photography Forward** — Tour cover images and destination photos carry emotional weight. Let images breathe with proper aspect ratios and subtle overlays — never crop them into tiny thumbnails.

### Component Patterns

- **Cards**: White surface, 18-22px radius, subtle border + shadow, image-forward
- **Navigation**: Glassmorphic (`backdrop-blur-2xl`), sticky, orange active states
- **Badges**: Pill-shaped, tinted backgrounds matching category color
- **Buttons**: Subtle hover lift (`translateY(-1px)`), orange glow shadow, scale-down on press
- **Overlays**: Bottom sheets on mobile, centered modals on desktop
- **Animations**: Spring physics for 3D tilt, `prefers-reduced-motion` respected everywhere
